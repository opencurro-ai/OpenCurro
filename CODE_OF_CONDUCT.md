OpenCurro AI Code of Conduct

Our Pledge

Like the technical community as a whole, the OpenCurro AI community is made up of professionals, contributors, and volunteers from around the world who collaborate on every aspect of the project, including development, mentorship, documentation, support, and community engagement.

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual orientation, or any other protected characteristic.

We believe that diversity strengthens our community and encourages innovation. We ask all participants to help create a respectful, collaborative, and supportive environment.

Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by the OpenCurro AI project or its maintainers. This includes, but is not limited to:

- GitHub repositories
- Issues
- Pull Requests
- GitHub Discussions
- Project documentation
- Community chat platforms
- Social media managed by the project
- Email communications
- Community events
- Any other official communication channels maintained by the project

Behavior outside these spaces may also be considered if it has a significant impact on the safety, well-being, or reputation of the OpenCurro AI community.

Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Be friendly and patient.
- Be welcoming. Support people of all backgrounds and experience levels.
- Be considerate. Remember that your work affects other contributors and users.
- Be respectful. Disagreements are natural, but personal attacks and hostility are never acceptable.
- Be professional. Communicate clearly, constructively, and respectfully.
- Be willing to learn. Accept feedback gracefully and help others improve.
- When we disagree, try to understand why. Focus on solving problems instead of blaming people.

Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are unacceptable and will not be tolerated:

- Harassment, intimidation, or bullying.
- Violent threats or abusive language.
- Hate speech or discriminatory comments.
- Personal attacks or insults.
- Sexually explicit or violent content.
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Posting or threatening to post another person's private information ("doxing").
- Deliberately disrupting discussions or community activities.
- Encouraging or advocating any of the behaviors listed above.
- Repeated harassment after being asked to stop.

Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, pull requests, discussions, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct. They may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation when necessary to protect the community.

Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by emailing:

opencurro@outlook.com

When making a report, please include as much relevant information as possible so the maintainers can investigate appropriately.

All reports will be reviewed promptly, handled fairly, and kept as confidential as reasonably possible.

Retaliation against anyone who reports a violation in good faith will not be tolerated.

Questions

If you have any questions about this Code of Conduct, please contact:

opencurro@outlook.com
